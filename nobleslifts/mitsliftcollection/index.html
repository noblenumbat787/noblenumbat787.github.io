Konnichiwa! Welcome!

This site is an online repository for Mitsubishi lifts of all speeds, shapes and sizes.

First off, yes, Mitsubishi (yes, that Mitsubishi - the one who also makes cars^), makes lifts and escalators too.

Read on to know more about Mitsubishi's presence in the lift industry:

"the Americans invented the lift; the Japanese perfected it."

Mitsubishi Electric makes lifts and escalators under the subsidiary Mitsubishi Electric Building Solutions Corporation.
Although formed in 1954, Mitsubishi was involved in the lift and escalator business since 1931, having initally had an technical agreement with Westinghouse of USA, till around the 1970s.

Mitsubishi's lifts are well-known around the world for their innovation, as well as their excellent craftsmanship and durability, with many units surviving decades.
Mitsubishi, though not being the first in the lift and escalator business, has been credited for many innovations in the domain.
For instance; their lifts have broken records thrice for being the fastest in the world:

  • Sunshine 60 (60 floors; Japan, 1978) - 600 m/min (10 m/s) both ways.
  • Yokohama Landmark Tower (73 floors; Japan, 1993) - 750 m/min (12.5 m/s) both ways.
  • Shanghai Tower (128 floors; China, 2015) - 1080-1230 m/min (18-20.5 m/s) up; 600 m/min (10 m/s) down.

For context, the lifts in the tallest building in the world - the Burj Khalifa in the UAE (163 floors, 830 m; opened in 2010) are only 10 m/s (both ways) Otis lifts up to the observation deck on floor 124.
Mitsubishi is only one of the few lift companies who have been able to make lifts capable of going beyond 10 m/s.
