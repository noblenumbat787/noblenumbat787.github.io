Mitsubishi lifts from GPS onwards (circa 1992) have voice announcements as a feature. Two options are offered:
AAN-B (Basic Announcement) AAN-G (Voice Guidance System)
Only special announcements included.
(e.g. "Please press the button for the desired floor.") 		Full announcement set,
including guidance as well as special announcements.
(e.g. "First floor")
Originally, voice announcements, either AAN-B or AAN-G; were offered as an optional feature by Mitsubishi.
However, on recent iterations of NexWay and ELEMOTION (as well as certain NEXIEZ models) come with AAN-B as standard issue; with the AAN-G (full announcement set) still left as an optional feature (see below table).


Applies to NexWay. Key: S - Standard; O - Optional
The three columns to the right denote the availability for a certain group control system type. Some features may be standard-issue or not available on certain group control systems.

(Source: NexWay brochure, Mitsubishi Electric Building Solutions, 2022.)

The announcement voices do at times vary from lift to lift.
Often, this is mostly due to regional localisation. Announcements in the English language are available in different accents depending upon the region.
These regional localisations include, but are not limited to:
  1. English (US)
  2. English (UK)
  3. English (Indonesian)
  4. English (Thai)
  5. English (Singapore)
Aside from accents, in certain cases the terms used also differ:
(e.g. "elevator" instead of "lift" in American English; "storey" instead of "floor" in Singaporean English)

This difference at times can lead to somewhat weird-sounding messages; such as:
US / UK English: Sorry, the floor selected is a restricted floor.
Singaporean English: Sorry, the storey selected is a restricted storey.

Also, in the American English locale, the voices differ between lifts equipped with AAN-B and AAN-G.

On lifts equipped with an LCD car position indicator; for most messages, the display will show a message alongside the aural announcement.

Identifying if a Mitsubishi lift is equipped with voice announcements

For a Mitsubishi lift equipped with AAN-G, this is a no-brainer. If it announces the direction or the floor; it will have AAN-G voice announcements.

However on a lift equipped with AAN-B voice announcements; which only play in special circumstances, it can be hard to tell if the lift is equipped or not.
One way is to close the doors without selecting a floor; which plays the unselected floor message if the lift is equipped with atleast AAN-B voice announcements.
Even though that works 100% effectively; it may not be practical to perform at all times.

Thus, another way to tell is to observe if the lift makes a "ding" chime upon closing the doors:

If so; then most likely the lift is equipped with AAN-B (or AAN-G) voice announcements. If not, the lift is not equipped with any voice announcments.
